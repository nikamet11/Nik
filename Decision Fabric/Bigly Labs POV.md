**Commercial Decision Intelligence (CDI)** roughly operates in three modes:

1. Owning strategic commercial decision domains end-to-end
2. Embedding data-driven decision-making into operational processes
3. Serving analytics needs that get surfaced from the business

Three strategic workstreams we are working on now are:

- **Promotional Excellence** — analytics-led promo planning and true promotional-effectiveness measurement
- **Assortment & Space** — data-led ranging and space optimisation
- **Vendor Engagement** — a value-led, differentiated vendor framework

...with the idea to expand this with **pricing optimisation** and **demand planning** in future

Alongside these we run **Decision-Driven Reporting**, where we are building reports for each vertical of the business, also varying dependent on the seniority of the decision makers that view these reports. The intent here is not to focus on ad hoc reporting, but rather what reports need to feed into standard decision-making points (e.g., Retail exec level reporting to be viewed monthly vs. store ops reports to be viewed weekly, all linked to pre-defined objectives of that business vertical). This is still very new, and we are focusing on the RetailOps, Commercial, and X, Bigly executive reporting to start.

Finally, the traditional analytics team also sits with me, where we serve more ad hoc analysis and reporting needs across the business.
