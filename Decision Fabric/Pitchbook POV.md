# Chapter 1 — Market Direction & Executive Imperative (2–3 slides)

This is your only "provocation" section. It should be lighter than Keppel but still establish urgency.

Possible governing thoughts:

- **Private market intelligence is becoming a competitive capability, not a research function.**
- **The speed of opportunity identification increasingly determines who wins mandates.**
- **Relationship banking is evolving into intelligence-enabled relationship banking.**

Support with market trends:

- Growth of private markets
- Rising competition for mandates
- Increasing expectations for faster, more tailored client insights
- AI accelerating information asymmetry

Then move on. Don't linger here.

# Chapter 2 — Institutionalizing PitchBook (5–6 slides)

This is where I think we should avoid explaining PitchBook itself. Instead, answer a more valuable executive question:

> **How do leading investment banks operationalize PitchBook into a repeatable origination capability?**

This chapter should be organized around a capability maturity journey, not product features.

### Governing Thought

**PitchBook delivers enterprise value only when embedded into front-office decision making.**

### The maturity journey

#### Phase 1 — Establish

**Create a trusted market intelligence foundation.**

Typical activities:

- Standardize data access
- Create research standards
- Identify priority business units
- Build taxonomy and governance

Output:

- Single source of private market intelligence

---

#### Phase 2 — Activate

**Embed intelligence into banker workflows.**

Use cases:

Corporate Banking

- Client profiling
- Relationship expansion

Investment Banking

- Comparable companies
- Buyer identification
- Seller screening
- M&A idea generation
- IPO pipeline

Private Equity Coverage

- Fund activity monitoring
- LP mapping
- Portfolio intelligence

Coverage Teams

- Sector monitoring
- Sponsor coverage
- Investor mapping

Business Development

- Target identification
- Cross-selling
- New relationship discovery

These align closely with PitchBook's own workflow categories—deal sourcing, market intelligence, networking, due diligence, fundraising, benchmarking, and business development.

---

#### Phase 3 — Industrialize

**Transform research into institutional intelligence.**

Now PitchBook becomes connected with:

- CRM
- Internal deal history
- Research
- AI copilots
- Knowledge repositories
- Workflow automation

Now bankers don't search.

They receive intelligence.

---

#### Phase 4 — Scale

The operating model expands across:

- Corporate Banking
- Investment Banking
- M&A Advisory
- Sponsor Coverage
- Capital Markets
- Strategy
- Risk
- Portfolio Management

This is where the value compounds because institutional knowledge is reused instead of recreated.

## The biggest shift I'd recommend

For every chapter, replace a descriptive title with a governing thought that could stand alone as an executive insight.

Instead of:

> "PitchBook Implementation Roadmap"

Use:

> **Competitive advantage is realized through operating model adoption—not platform deployment.**

Instead of:

> "Use Cases"

Use:

> **The highest returns come from embedding intelligence where bankers make decisions.**

Instead of:

> "Scaling"

Use:

> **Institutional intelligence compounds when every engagement strengthens the next.**

Those are the kinds of declarative headings that make a deck feel like it came from an executive strategy team rather than a technology implementation partner. They tell the audience _what to believe_, not just _what they'll see next_.
